# Client FAQ

Pre-written answers in coaching voice — for fast, consistent responses to common questions.

---

**"What if I'm not hungry for breakfast?"**
That's fine — there's nothing magic about breakfast specifically. What matters is hitting your totals by the end of the day. If skipping breakfast means you're starving and overeat later, that's the thing to watch for, not the skipping itself.

**"Can I drink alcohol?"**
Yes, in moderation, and budgeted into your day like any other calorie source. It won't directly hurt progress on occasion — frequent or heavy drinking is a different conversation, and we can talk specifics if that's relevant to you.

**"Do I need a cheat meal?"**
You don't *need* one, but having planned flexibility (a meal out, a dessert, whatever) tends to make a goal sustainable longer than treating every day identically. The key word is planned — it's part of the plan, not a reaction to restriction.

**"Why am I not losing weight even though I'm 'eating healthy'?"**
"Healthy" foods still have calories. Olive oil, nuts, avocado, granola — all great foods, all calorie-dense. This is almost always a portion/volume issue, not a food-quality issue. Let's look at your actual tracked numbers together.

**"Do I have to track forever?"**
No — tracking is a learning tool, not a life sentence. Most people graduate to eyeballing portions accurately after a few consistent weeks of tracking. We'll figure out together when you're ready to loosen that.

**"What if I go over my calories one day?"**
One day doesn't undo progress. Get back to your normal pattern the next meal — no need to "make up for it" by undereating later, that just creates a binge/restrict cycle.

**"Can I still eat [specific food they love]?"**
Almost certainly yes, in some amount. The conversation is "how does this fit," not "is this allowed."

**"Why protein specifically — why not just count calories?"**
Calories determine whether you gain/lose/maintain weight. Protein determines *what kind* of weight that is — keeping muscle while losing fat, or building muscle efficiently while gaining. Both matter, they just do different jobs.
