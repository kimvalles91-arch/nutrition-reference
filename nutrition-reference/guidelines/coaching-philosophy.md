# Coaching Philosophy — Internal Reference

Not client-facing. This is the "how I think" doc — useful for staying consistent across clients, and essential if a second coach ever joins.

---

## Core principle
Teach clients to understand protein, carbs, fats, fiber, and micronutrients well enough to build their own meals — not hand them a meal plan. The plan is temporary; the understanding is permanent.

## Calorie & macro target logic
1. Estimate maintenance via Mifflin-St Jeor + activity multiplier (see onboarding tool — this is automated as a starting point, not gospel).
2. Adjust by goal:
   - Fat loss: -15 to -20% from maintenance
   - Recomp: -10%
   - Muscle gain: +10%
   - Performance/maintenance: no adjustment, focus on nutrient timing/quality instead
3. Protein target: ~1g per lb bodyweight as a default starting point. Adjust down slightly (~0.8g/lb) for clients at higher bodyweights where 1g/lb would be unreasonably high relative to lean mass.
4. Fat: ~25% of total calories as a default floor — adjust up for clients who report better satiety/hormonal feeling on higher fat, down (not below ~20%) for clients who prioritize carbs for training performance.
5. Carbs: remainder. This is intentional — carbs are the flexible macro, used to fill out calories around training demands.

## Adjusting over time
- Reassess every 2-4 weeks based on actual progress (weight trend, performance, adherence, hunger).
- Don't chase weekly fluctuations — water weight and other noise are real.
- If adherence is consistently poor, the problem is usually the target/structure, not the client's discipline. Simplify before tightening.

## Handling eating out
Don't ask clients to avoid restaurants. Teach: protein-forward ordering, sauces/dressings on the side, fibrous veg sides over starchy ones when trying to stay leaner that meal, and accepting some imprecision is fine.

## Handling plateaus
1. Confirm tracking accuracy first (this is usually the actual issue, not metabolism).
2. Check for any "ate-out-but-didn't-log" gaps.
3. Reassess maintenance — bodyweight changes shift TDEE over time.
4. Only then consider adjusting targets.

## Red flags to watch for
- Client describing intense guilt/anxiety around specific foods → may be straying into disordered patterns, soften the macro-tracking emphasis and check in directly.
- Rapid, unsustainable restriction the client self-imposed beyond what was recommended → address directly, don't just let it ride because "they're getting results."
- Client asking for increasingly extreme restriction → this is a conversation, not a programming adjustment.

## Voice/tone for all client-facing material
- Plain language, no jargon without explanation
- Non-judgmental about food choices — no "good/bad" framing
- Always tie a recommendation back to "why," not just "what"
