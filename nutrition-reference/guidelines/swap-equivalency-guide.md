# Swap & Equivalency Guide

The point of this doc: give clients a mental model for substituting foods *within* a category without throwing off their macros. This is the core of "teach, don't prescribe."

---

## Protein swaps (~roughly equal per serving)
One serving ≈ 25-30g protein:
- 4 oz chicken breast
- 4 oz lean beef (93/7)
- 4 oz white fish or shrimp
- 4 oz salmon (higher fat — adjust fat elsewhere if swapping in)
- 1 cup Greek yogurt
- 3/4 cup cottage cheese
- 1 scoop whey or plant protein
- 3-4 whole eggs + a couple egg whites

**Coaching note:** fish/salmon and eggs carry more fat per serving than chicken/turkey/white fish — if a client swaps a lean protein for a fattier one, that's calories added, not a 1-for-1 trade. Worth pointing out once so they start noticing it themselves.

## Starchy carb swaps (~roughly equal per serving)
One serving ≈ 35-45g carbs:
- 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
- 1 medium potato or sweet potato
- 1 cup cooked pasta
- 1/2 cup dry oats
- 1 cup cooked quinoa
- 1 large tortilla or 2 small
- 1 bagel (roughly 1.5-2 servings — bagels run big)

## Fibrous veg — basically free to swap
Any vegetable on the fibrous-carb list can sub for another with negligible macro impact. The teaching point here: **volume matters for fullness, calories barely move.** This is the category to lean on when a client says they're hungry but still in their calories.

## Fat swaps (~roughly equal per serving)
One serving ≈ 14g fat:
- 1 tbsp olive oil, butter, or coconut oil
- 1/2 medium avocado
- 1 oz nuts (~20-24 almonds, ~14 walnut halves)
- 2 tbsp nut butter (note: nut butter also carries protein/carbs, not pure fat)

**Coaching note:** fats are the easiest category to overshoot because they're calorie-dense in small volume. "Eyeballing" a fat serving is the riskiest of the three macros — encourage measuring (tbsp, not "a drizzle") until it's intuitive.

## Fruit swaps (~roughly equal per serving)
One serving ≈ 15-25g carbs:
- 1 cup berries
- 1 medium banana, apple, or pear
- 1 cup grapes, mango, or pineapple
- 1/4 cup dried fruit (dried fruit is calorie-dense — small volume packs a lot)

---

## How to teach this in a session
1. Start with their plate as a whole, not individual foods: "this meal is roughly a palm of protein, a fist of carbs, a thumb of fat, and as many veggies as you want."
2. Once they're comfortable eyeballing portions, introduce specific swaps from this list for foods they want variety on.
3. Revisit every few weeks — as their calorie target changes with progress, the *swap logic* doesn't change, only the *serving sizes* do. That consistency is the whole point.
